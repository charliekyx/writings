# Article angle (after research)

1–2 pages: title direction, H2 structure, must-cover keywords and anxiety points. No component specs.

- **Title direction:**  
  Lead with corridor + cost transparency. Examples: *Corridor Audit: MYR → USD via IBKR — Real Cost Breakdown* or *MYR to IBKR: Hidden Fees, Spread, and What You Actually Get*. Include at least one of: real cost / hidden fees / spread; and MYR (or Malaysia) + IBKR (or Interactive Brokers).

- **H2 structure (suggested):**  
  1. **Real cost formula** — True cost = nominal fees + FX spread + intermediary/wire cost + time. (Address anxiety: “what am I really paying?”)  
  2. **Fees and spread** — Break down IBKR + bank/Wise + SWIFT or wire; compare to “mid” or reference rate.  
  3. **MYR → USD route in practice** — One or two H2s on the actual path (e.g. SWIFT vs Wise), with numbers where possible.  
  4. **What you actually receive** — Exact delta / to-the-cent framing (align with calculator angle without specifying component).  
  Optional: short H2 or callout on “why this isn’t a step-by-step guide” (performance benchmark, not tutorial).

- **Must-cover keywords:**  
  IBKR, Malaysia / Malaysian, MYR, USD, fees, transfer, deposit, Wise, bank, exchange rate, spread, hidden (cost/fees), wire, SWIFT. Use “real cost” and “what you actually get” early. Weave in “intermediary” and “time” in the formula section.

- **Anxiety points to address (from anxiety_checklist):**  
  - “Hidden” costs and spread (supported by data — lead here).  
  - SWIFT / wire and bank fees (supported).  
  - Real cost vs. advertised rate (supported; use your formula).  
  - Intermediary cost (weaker in search — explain in body/formula, not necessarily in title).  
  - Position piece as independent audit / benchmark, not an how-to, to match audience and differentiate from generic tutorials.
